What is a Tequeño?

Our best-selling product is a Tequeño, and it's not hard to guess why. Tequeños are crunchy and delicious and a true crowd pleaser at parties and events. You may be wondering, however, what is a tequeño? Below, we outline the interesting history of tequeños.

Tequeño are small cheese sticks rolled in soft dough.

Origin

Preparation

A tequeño is a popular meal and snack in Venezuela but the exact origin of its name is unclear. It is most commonly believed that the snack is named after Los Teques, and was perhaps invented in the kitchen of an affluent family living in Los Teques.

To prepare a tequeño, soft dough is wrapped around a stick of cheese (in its original version) and shaped into a breadstick. The sticks are then fried in oil until golden brown.

Versatility

Did you know?

Tequeños are an incredibly versatile snack and can be eaten for breakfast, as a snack, as a starter or side dish. They are perfect for parties, wedding and corporate events when you want to serve snacks that are equal parts delicious and filling.

There are many different recipes for tequeños, but we believe we have truly mastered the art of the tequeño. Tequeños can be made with multiple fillings, but we have found salty, white cheese to be the most popular. Tequeños are quickly gaining popularity in the UK and are regularly enjoyed in Venezuela, Latin America and Spain.
